## PIRs
1. Has the enemy conducted reconnaissance on the network?
2. Has the enemy acquired or developed resources (tools, infrastructure, or accounts) to support an attack against the network?
3. Has the enemy gained initial access to any host or system on the network?
4. Has the enemy executed malicious code or commands on any compromised host?
5. Has the enemy established persistence mechanisms on any compromised host to maintain long-term access?
6. Has the enemy attempted to escalate privileges on any system within the network?
7. Has the enemy taken steps to evade detection or disable security controls and monitoring?
8. Has the enemy attempted to access, steal, or brute-force credentials from any system or credential store?
9. Has the enemy conducted internal discovery to enumerate network topology, assets, or user accounts?
10. Has the enemy attempted to move laterally to other systems or segments within the network?
11. Has the enemy collected sensitive data or staged files from compromised systems for exfiltration?
12. Has the enemy established command and control (C2) communications with any internal host?
13. Has the enemy exfiltrated data outside the network boundary?
14. Has the enemy attempted to disrupt, degrade, destroy, or manipulate systems, services, or data?
